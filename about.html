Background

I started in analytics-focused consulting at Princeton Consultants, then moved to BCG, where I worked on strategy and operations cases across healthcare, retail, and financial services. The common thread was translating analysis into recommendations that executives could act on.

At Red Ventures, I applied that approach to building and managing a digital marketing product for healthcare providers. I owned the P&L from concept through $15M in annual revenue—pricing models, client renewals, and targeting algorithms. The role required forecasting accuracy, margin discipline, and close collaboration with sales and product teams.

I'm now based in Little Rock and focused on FP&A and strategic finance roles where I can contribute to operating cadence, planning processes, and executive reporting.

How I Work

  • Driver-based modeling: Financial models structured around operational assumptions, not top-down extrapolations. Clear inputs, traceable outputs.
  • Variance analysis: Decomposition by price, volume, and mix with reconciling bridges. Focus on isolating actionable drivers.
  • Scenario planning: Multiple scenarios with explicit assumptions. Sensitivity analysis on the variables that matter most.
  • Executive communication: Board decks and management packs designed for decision-making. Lead with takeaways, support with detail.

I approach decisions through expected value and sensitivity analysis—understanding which assumptions drive the answer, and where the model is most uncertain.
