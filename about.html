The Path Here

I started as a math nerd at Duke, fell into consulting at Princeton Consultants (where half the staff had PhDs), and eventually made my way to BCG. There I learned how to structure problems, stress-test assumptions, and communicate with executives who had about 30 seconds of patience.

At Red Ventures, I built something from scratch: a digital marketing product for healthcare providers that went from whiteboard concept to $15M in revenue in three years. I owned the P&L, built the pricing models, managed client renewals, and created the targeting algorithms. 100% renewal rate. Third-party verified 15x ROI.

That experience crystallized what I love most: the messy middle between strategy and execution where financial models actually hit reality.

What I Believe About FP&A

  • Driver-based beats line-item. If your budget is "last year plus 3%," you're not planning — you're guessing.
  • Accuracy matters, but usefulness matters more. A directionally-right forecast delivered Tuesday beats a perfect one delivered Friday.
  • AI is a tool, not magic. ML improves forecasts 20%+ in the right contexts. It's useless in others. Knowing the difference is the skill.
  • The "so what?" is everything. If your variance report doesn't end with a recommendation, it's just arithmetic.
