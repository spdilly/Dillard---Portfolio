Behavioral Health Operating Review and FP&A Model

Simulated Company · Full Year Analysis · January 2025

Context

Clearpath Behavioral Health is a simulated intensive outpatient program (IOP) operator in Arkansas with two clinic locations. The company missed its FY1 revenue budget by 6% and EBITDA by 15%, requiring analysis of root causes and strategic recommendations for FY2.

This case study demonstrates the full FP&A toolkit: variance decomposition, driver based modeling, scenario analysis, and executive ready reporting.

Problem

  • Revenue missed budget by $296K (6%), driven by volume and payer mix headwinds
  • EBITDA margin compressed from 29.6% target to 26.8% actual
  • Leadership needed clarity on root causes and quantified path to recovery
  • Board requested scenario analysis with probability weighted expectations

Approach

  • Built driver based P&L model connecting operating metrics (sessions, show rate, payer mix) to financial outcomes
  • Decomposed revenue variance into volume, rate, and mix components with reconciling bridges
  • Constructed three scenarios (downside, base, upside) with explicit driver assumptions
  • Quantified four initiatives with defensible ROI calculations
  • Packaged findings in BCG style operating review deck for board presentation

Key Findings

  • Volume drove two thirds of revenue miss: 1,018 fewer sessions delivered, split between admissions shortfall and show rate deterioration (88% vs 90% target)
  • Payer mix shift reduced blended rate: 2 point drift toward Medicaid reduced average reimbursement by $5/session
  • Cost discipline preserved margin: $75K favorable variance from delayed hiring and lower variable costs offset 25% of revenue shortfall
  • Show rate intervention offers highest ROI: 2 point improvement yields $40K EBITDA with minimal investment

Outputs

  • 10 slide operating review deck with revenue and EBITDA bridges
  • Driver based Excel model with scenario toggles and sensitivity analysis
  • FY2 forecast with probability weighted EBITDA expectation
  • Initiative roadmap with owners, timelines, and quantified impact

A Note on Methodology

Clearpath is a simulated company. The data was generated programmatically using Python to create a realistic but fictional behavioral health operator. All numbers are derived from a small set of core assumptions (staffing, reimbursement rates, show rates) that cascade through the model deterministically.

This approach ensures every variance bridge balances, every reconciliation foots, and the story coheres. The methodology deck (linked above) explains the data generation process in detail, including the Python code patterns used.

Industry benchmarks for reimbursement rates, staffing ratios, and margins are based on real world data from CMS fee schedules, SAMHSA reports, and healthcare M&A comps.
