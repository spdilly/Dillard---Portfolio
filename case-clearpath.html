Behavioral Health Operating Review

Simulated Company · Full Year Analysis · January 2025

Context & Objective

Clearpath Behavioral Health is a simulated intensive outpatient program operator in Arkansas with two clinic locations and approximately $5M in annual revenue. The company missed its FY1 revenue budget by 6% and EBITDA by 15%.

The objective was to identify root causes through variance decomposition, model recovery scenarios, and deliver a board-ready operating review with quantified recommendations.

What I Did

  • Built a driver-based P&L model connecting operational metrics (sessions delivered, show rate, payer mix) to financial outcomes
  • Decomposed revenue variance into volume, rate, and mix components with reconciling bridges
  • Constructed three scenarios (downside, base, upside) with explicit driver assumptions and probability weighting
  • Quantified four strategic initiatives with ROI calculations and implementation timelines
  • Packaged findings into an executive operating review deck structured for board presentation
  • Documented methodology and data generation approach for transparency
Tools: Excel (driver-based model, variance bridges), Python (data generation), PowerPoint

Results

  • Volume drove two-thirds of revenue shortfall: 1,018 fewer sessions than plan, split between admissions shortfall and show rate deterioration
  • Payer mix shift reduced blended rate: 2-point drift toward Medicaid reduced average reimbursement by $5/session
  • Cost discipline offset 25% of shortfall: $75K favorable variance from delayed hiring
  • Show rate intervention identified as highest-ROI lever: 2-point improvement yields ~$40K EBITDA with minimal investment

Note on Data

Clearpath is a simulated company. The underlying data was generated programmatically to create a realistic but fictional behavioral health operator. Reimbursement rates are based on CMS fee schedules; staffing ratios and margins reflect industry benchmarks from healthcare M&A comparables.

All variance bridges reconcile, and scenarios flow from explicit driver assumptions.
