Pricing Sensitivity & Segmentation

3S Home Services · PPL Pricing Model · April 2023

Context & Objective

3S Home Services operates a lead generation platform for home improvement contractors in the roofing vertical. The company sells leads on a pay-per-lead (PPL) basis, with pricing intended to reflect downstream conversion value.

The objective was to develop a data-driven pricing framework that segments leads by quality signals and supports defensible rate adjustments with partners.

What I Did

  • Built PPL pricing formula: Lead Value = Appointment Rate × Close Rate × Average Sale × Desired Margin
  • Analyzed 16 months of lead and appointment data to identify conversion patterns by source
  • Segmented leads by keyword intent (local vs. generic) to isolate quality signals
  • Mapped channel and geographic performance to identify pricing and allocation opportunities
  • Developed tiered pricing structure with built-in data collection for ongoing refinement
  • Documented data limitations and built conservative assumptions into recommendations
Tools: Excel (pricing model, funnel analysis), SQL (data extraction)

Results

  • Premium PPL supported at $90: Conservative pricing based on 8.4% appointment rate and 39% close rate (Q3 data showed 13% appointment rates)
  • Local keywords convert 38% higher: 11% vs. 8% appointment rate, supporting $110 PPL for local segment vs. $80 for generic
  • Tiered pricing adds ~$30K incremental revenue: Same lead volume, higher average yield through quality-based pricing
  • Seasonality risk flagged: Heavy Q3 weighting in appointment data; recommended rapid adjustment capability for Q1

Technical Notes

The pricing formula is standard for performance marketing contexts. The key insight was that appointment rate varies significantly by lead source, making segmented pricing more accurate than flat rates.

The analysis explicitly flagged data limitations: Q3 weighting in appointment data, potential lag in partner reporting, and geographic concentration risk. The recommended $90 PPL is conservative relative to peak-season data to build margin of safety while collecting more granular conversion data.
