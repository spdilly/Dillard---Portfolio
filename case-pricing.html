Pricing Sensitivity & Segmentation

3S Home Services · PPL Pricing Model · April 2023

Context

3S Home Services operates a lead generation platform for home improvement contractors, primarily in the roofing vertical. The business sells leads on a pay-per-lead (PPL) basis, where pricing depends on lead quality as measured by downstream conversion to appointments and sales.

This analysis developed a data-driven pricing framework to optimize PPL rates across different lead segments, balancing revenue capture with partner retention.

Problem

  • Flat PPL pricing left money on the table for high-quality lead segments
  • Appointment rate data showed significant variance by keyword type, channel, and season
  • Partner needed defensible pricing methodology to justify rate increases
  • Limited historical data created uncertainty around conversion assumptions

Approach

  • Built PPL pricing formula: Lead × Appointment Rate × Conversion × Average Sale × Margin
  • Analyzed 16 months of lead and appointment data to identify conversion patterns
  • Segmented leads by keyword intent (local vs. generic) to isolate quality signals
  • Mapped channel and geographic performance to identify expansion opportunities
  • Developed tiered pricing structure with built-in data collection mechanisms

Key Findings

  • Premium PPL achievable: $90 flat rate supported by 8.4% appointment rate and 39% conversion; conservative vs. Q3 data showing 13% appointment rates
  • Local keywords convert 38% higher: 11% vs. 8% appointment rate, supporting $110 PPL for local segment vs. $80 for generic
  • Segmented pricing adds $30K revenue: Incremental value from quality-based tiering vs. flat rate
  • Seasonality risk requires monitoring: Q3 skew in appointment data; rapid adjustment capability needed
  • Channel optimization opportunity: Organic and social outperform paid search; investment reallocation recommended

Technical Notes

The pricing formula used here is standard for performance marketing: PPL = Lead × Appointment Rate × Conversion Rate × Average Sale × Desired Margin. The key insight is that appointment rate varies significantly by lead source, making segmented pricing more accurate than flat rates.

The analysis flagged data limitations explicitly: heavy Q3 weighting in appointment data, potential lag in partner reporting, and geographic concentration risk. The recommended $90 PPL is conservative vs. peak season data specifically to build in margin of safety while collecting more precise data.
